Learning A New Language

Python’s expansive library of powerful open source data analysis tools, web frameworks, and dynamic qualities make it one of the most popular and in-demand languages in the field of computer science. This high level programming language has gained so much popularity and praise in the IT world, partly because it is extremely simple and easy to learn. The code reads like the English language, thus taking far less time to get a handle on compared to many other languages.

However, there still needs to be some structure to learn and fully comprehend the language. And that is where these tutorials come into play.

Being a self-taught programmer, I have gone through numerous websites and tutorials in an attempt to find the perfect one that would teach me nearly everything I would need to know, or at least lay the building blocks. However, whenever I’d thought I had found something good, I realize that I was missing out on a chunk of what makes this language so great.

For that reason, I have put a lot of effort into making sure all aspects of basic Python programming are met and here for you to freely access.

Regardless, remember that despite reading through all the content provided on this site, you will most likely not reach your full potential in the language. It is of utmost importance that you put these lessons into practice. To guide you in the right direction in terms of practice, make sure to check out the Practice section. I also recommend that once you get a handle on the main ideas, that you start some bigger project. For more info on future projects visit these sites:

  • Five mini programming projects for the Python beginner
  • 8 Projects You Can Build to Learn Python in 2020
  • 6 Python Projects For Beginners
  • 42 Exciting Python Project Ideas & Topics for Beginners [2020]

Finally, even though many say that Python is one of the easiest, yet powerful languages out there, it can still be challenging at times. I sometimes stumble across obstacles and then resolve those, and that's the joy of programming. There could be instances where you stumble over something you may want to try out for yourself, but then struggle to get the code to run on your device. It may take up some time to get a hold of the syntax and soon may even bore you, leading you to begin to lose interest and motivation to keep going. At times like these, don’t spend longer than fifteen minutes on a single problem; just take a break and come back to it sometime later. This way, you’ll keep yourself more engaged and get better results with the time you put in.
