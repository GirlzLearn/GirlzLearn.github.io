What Is Programming

...and why is it so important?

      Before immersing ourselves in the language of Python, it is important to understand what programming really is.

Put simply, programming is a way for the user to communicate with a computer to get some sort of output. I like to think of four main steps when it comes to programming (FANG):

  • Figuring out the problem

What is it that you are trying to solve? This may seem redundant and unnecessary, but it's important to figure out simply what you want the computer to do and what output you may be expecting from the user.

  • Assess what needs to be done to solve this problem

What’s the solution? Or, how are you planning on solving the problem? Similar to the above situation, the more complicated and in depth your problems get, once you understand the problem, it is vital to understand how you are planning solve the problem.

  • Now, take this solution and transfer into code so that the computer understands

Time for the fun part! This will most likely be the longest step in your process: taking this solution you’ve come up with and transforming it into a language that the computer can comprehend.

  • Get your output and celebrate!

Time to run your code… and it’s successful! Go celebrate while the excitement lasts because soon enough you’ll be repeating the process again with some new prompt!
