Why do these Practice Prompts?

    Hopefully, by now you have a basic understanding in programming with Python through reading over various tutorials. Hopefully, you have also played around with what I have shown in my tutorials on your console. However, you will not get the full understanding of how to code and the logic that you should apply until you actually practice.

    To the left, I have put up several beginner level exercise prompts that I suggest you take a look at. Except for the first couple, all the prompts under "Beginnings" do not require any deep knowledge in Python. All you really need is to bring a fresh mind ready to think and put your common skills as well as some basic math knowledge to the test.

    In regards to pushing your knowledge in Python a bit further, I recommend you visit my User Input lesson and try to implement that into your prompts. Also, keep in mind that Python is all about automating, completing repetitive tasks effortlessly with the aid of this language. For those reasons, try your best to keep your programs simple, easy to read, and efficient.

    My final note is to say that some prompts may seem harder to accomplish than others and that is completely fine. Since most of these prompts are in relation to some level of mathematics, I highly suggest that if you feel stuck on what to do next, trace your steps back to the mathematical aspect of it all. Think of what you would do if you are given this same problem, just in your math class. How would you go about solving the problem? You can even write your program on paper if need be, I know it tends to help sometimes. After that, hopefully you will start to see a clearer picture and get to coding. If you are still unsure and need a little push to get you going, you can take a quick look at the provided answer, or even ask any friends if they are also learning the course.

Good Luck and have fun coding!!!

I would like to reiterate that most program/prompts provided are inspired by those offered on a site by Michigan State University. The sample answers I have provided are those that I came up with. Only the prompts are taken from the MSU website.
